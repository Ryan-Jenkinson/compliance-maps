Overview

The Andersen Compliance Intelligence Dashboard is an automated regulatory monitoring tool that aggregates, filters, and summarizes developments across seven active regulatory areas relevant to Andersen's manufacturing operations: PFAS, EPR, REACH, TSCA, Conflict Minerals, Forced Labor, and Prop 65.

The system scrapes 17 federal and state sources daily, runs a three-stage Claude AI pipeline to filter noise and generate company-specific analysis, then renders a live dashboard and weekly email briefing. Everything is weighted for Andersen's specific exposure — direct product risk (fluoropolymer coatings) and indirect supply chain risk (components, o-rings, equipment, MRO).

Search & Filters

Search Bar

The search bar at the top of the main content area searches article headlines, summaries, and source names in real time. Results are filtered as you type — no page reload required. Press / from anywhere on the page to jump focus to the search field instantly.

  • Press Esc to clear the search and return to the full article list
  • Searches are case-insensitive and match partial words
  • Search for a supplier name (e.g., "3M", "Chemours") to find compliance news related to that supplier
  • Search for a chemical name (e.g., "PTFE", "PFOA") to filter all articles mentioning it across topics

Deep Dive Button

When you type a search term, a Deep Dive button appears next to the search bar. Clicking it sends your search term to the AI analysis pipeline and opens a full, AI-generated analysis page for that topic in a new tab — including source context, company impact, and strategic recommendations.

★
Deep Dive is most useful for tracking a specific chemical, regulation, or jurisdiction (e.g., "Minnesota PFAS", "EPR packaging", "PFAS in coatings"). The AI has full context of Andersen's product portfolio.

Filter Buttons

Each topic section has a row of filter buttons below the section header. These narrow the article list within that topic:

  • All — show all articles for this topic
  • New — only articles first seen in this pipeline run
  • HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW — filter by urgency tier
  • Direct — articles with direct product impact
  • Indirect — articles affecting supply chain but not direct products

Date Range Filter

A date range picker at the top of the article grid lets you narrow the displayed articles to a specific time window. This is useful for reviewing what was happening around a specific regulatory deadline or during a particular quarter.

Relevance Badges

  • NEW — This article appeared in the pipeline for the first time. Articles that recur across pipeline runs show no badge ("carried over").
  • INDIRECT — The regulatory development affects Andersen's supply chain (suppliers, components, MRO) but does not directly apply to Andersen's manufactured products.
  • MONITOR — General regulatory news. No immediate company impact, but worth watching. Common for early-stage rulemakings.

Urgency Badges

  • HIGH — Active enforcement, a final rule, or a deadline arriving within 90 days. Action required or being tracked actively.
  • MEDIUM — Proposed rule, comment period open, or a deadline 90–365 days out. Requires monitoring and preparation.
  • LOW — Long-range rulemaking, informational notice, or a deadline more than 1 year away. Background awareness only.

Impact Score

The circular number in the top-right of each card is the company-specific impact score (1–10). This is not a general news importance score — it reflects Andersen's specific exposure to this development, weighted across four factors:

20%

  • 7+  High company impact — flag for leadership discussion
  • 4–6  Moderate impact — monitor and track in compliance log
  • 1–3  Low impact — background awareness, no action needed
⚠
A HIGH urgency badge and a high impact score are independent signals. An article can be HIGH urgency (fast-moving deadline) with a low impact score (applies to a different industry). Always read both together.

Executive Briefing

The Executive Briefing appears at the top of the main content area, above the topic article feeds. It is generated by Claude Sonnet as the final stage of the daily pipeline run.

Structure

The briefing has three named parts:

  1. Opening — one-paragraph summary of the overall compliance posture for the current week. Reads as a "here's where we stand" to lead a team meeting.
  2. Key Developments — bulleted list of the most material regulatory changes in the rolling window. Each bullet names the regulation, the development, and the Andersen-specific implication.
  3. Outlook — what to watch in the coming weeks. Flags known upcoming deadlines, comment periods closing, or expected rule publications.

The briefing ends with a Fun Fact — a brief compliance-adjacent note to keep the weekly email from feeling like a wall of bad news.

Rolling Window

The briefing covers the last 7 days ending on the day of generation. Articles older than 5 days are marked "carried over" in the article feed — they appeared in a previous pipeline run but remain actively relevant. The AI is aware of carry-over status and does not re-surface old news as if it were new.

ℹ
The briefing is written for experts who already know the regulatory landscape. It does not re-explain background context, major agencies, or long-standing deadlines. It covers what changed in the last 7 days.

Topic Sections

The main content area is divided into per-topic article feed sections. Each section covers one regulatory area: PFAS, EPR, REACH, TSCA, Prop 65, Conflict Minerals, Forced Labor.

Command Strip

At the very top of the article area, a horizontal command strip shows summary chips for each topic. Each chip displays:

  • Total article count for that topic
  • HIGH urgency count (red chip — means action may be needed)
  • MEDIUM urgency count (amber chip)

Click any chip to jump directly to that topic's section. Red chips signal immediate review is warranted.

Topic Section Structure

Each topic section contains:

  • Section header — topic name, article count, and expand/collapse toggle
  • Filter buttons — All / New / HIGH / MED / LOW / Direct / Indirect
  • Article cards — sorted by urgency then recency by default
  • "Show History" button — loads articles older than the 30-day display window from the archive
  • Strategy note — AI-generated guidance at the bottom of each section, specific to that topic and Andersen's current position

Strategy Notes

The strategy note at the bottom of each topic section is produced by the same AI pipeline run. It synthesizes the week's developments into a concise action note: if there is something material to flag, it does so with strategic context. If nothing changed materially, it briefly acknowledges that and restates the current monitoring plan for that area.

★
Strategy notes are written for the compliance team, not beginners. They assume you know what PFAS is, what the MN PRISM deadline means, and where Andersen currently stands. They are intended to support meeting prep, not replace it.

Regulatory Calendar

The full-width calendar below the main grid shows all tracked deadlines as colored topic dots on their due dates. Features:

  • Click any day to see what deadlines or events fall on that date
  • Milestone deadlines (e.g., MN PRISM July 1, 2026) appear as highlighted markers with a label
  • TODAY button — jumps the calendar view to the current month
  • BILLS toggle — overlays bill activity markers (committee hearings, floor votes) on the calendar for a combined view
  • Color coding by topic — PFAS in teal, EPR in blue, etc. — matches the sidebar and article section colors
★
Subscribe to the .ics calendar (Resources panel > Subscribe .ics Calendar) to see all tracked compliance deadlines in your calendar app (Outlook, Google Calendar, Apple Calendar). The file updates with each pipeline run.

Legislative Activity

Legislative tracking covers active bills across all tracked topics — state and federal — with stage, jurisdiction, and urgency. Three panels contribute to the legislative picture:

Bill Activity Table

A table in the main content area lists active bills with:

  • Bill ID & title — linked to the source text when available
  • Topic — which regulatory area the bill relates to
  • Jurisdiction — state or federal
  • Stage — Introduced / Committee / Floor / Enacted
  • Urgency — HIGH/MED/LOW based on momentum and timeline
  • Last action date — when it last moved

Cross-State Intel Panel

Located in the right sidebar, the Cross-State Intel panel contains AI-synthesized analysis of what is moving across states, organized by topic tab. It includes:

  • A narrative summary of multi-state legislative trends (e.g., "8 states now have active PFAS in products bills with July 2026 reporting triggers")
  • Watch List — specific states flagged for near-term action based on bill momentum
  • Next Movers — AI predictions for which states are likely to advance legislation in the next 60–90 days, based on committee activity and co-sponsor patterns

What Changed Today

A compact panel (also in the right sidebar) summarizes new regulatory developments detected in the last 24 hours. This is the fastest way to see if anything material broke overnight before reviewing the full article feed. It is generated from the same scraper run and refreshes with each pipeline execution.

Right Sidebar Widgets

The right sidebar contains seven panels, stacked top to bottom. Each is a quick-reference view of a different data dimension.

Panel What it shows
Regulatory Status Traffic-light matrix: each topic × three dimensions (Activity level, High Urgency articles, Upcoming Deadlines). Red = active concern, amber = watch, green = stable.
What Changed Today 24-hour change log — new articles, stage changes in tracked bills, and newly published rules detected in the most recent pipeline run.
6-Month Trend Bar chart of article volume by topic over the past 6 months. Use this to spot if a topic is accelerating (e.g., PFAS volumes spiking in Q1 before a deadline).
30-Day Daily Activity Sparkline of total daily article count over the past 30 days. Useful for identifying cluster events (e.g., a court ruling or EPA announcement generating a burst of coverage).
Bill Pipeline Funnel Funnel visualization showing tracked bills by legislative stage: Introduced → Committee → Floor → Enacted. Shows how many bills are at each stage across all topics.
Resources Quick links: open topic timelines, download .xlsx trackers, download or subscribe to the .ics compliance calendar, access the historical archive.
Cross-State Intel AI-synthesized state legislative analysis by topic tab (see Section 8).
★
The Regulatory Status matrix is the fastest daily health check. If any row is all-green, that topic can be deprioritized this week. If PFAS shows red across all three columns, that drives the team meeting agenda.

Power Tips

🔍
Search by chemical name
Type "PTFE" or "PFOA" to instantly filter all articles mentioning that compound across every topic section.
🏭
Search by supplier name
Search for "3M", "Chemours", or any supplier to surface compliance news that might affect that part of your supply chain.
🔴
Red command strip chips
A red chip in the command strip at the top of the article area means HIGH urgency articles are present in that topic. Go there first.
📌
Carried-over articles
Articles without a NEW badge appeared in a previous pipeline run but are still actively relevant. They are not stale — they were deliberately retained.
📊
Impact score is Andersen-specific
A score of 8 means high impact for Andersen — direct product or supply chain exposure. It is not a measure of news importance to the industry generally.
📅
Subscribe to the .ics calendar
Resources panel > Subscribe .ics Calendar. Compliance deadlines will appear in Outlook or Google Calendar and update automatically.
🗺
State maps show live bill status
Maps group in the sidebar opens the PFAS and EPR state maps. States with active bills are shaded by stage — useful for quickly orienting on geographic exposure.
🤖
Director Review page
The Director Review entry in the left nav opens an AI critique of the dashboard's own coverage — useful for spotting blind spots or topics receiving insufficient attention.
📥
Download .xlsx trackers
Resources panel > Downloads. Each topic has a structured Excel tracker suitable for sharing outside the dashboard environment.
