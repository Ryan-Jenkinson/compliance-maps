● Breaking
EPA's March 25 announcement extending compliance dates for perchloroethylene and carbon tetrachloride TSCA rules is the most actionable item this week — it buys time but does not eliminate the obligation, and companies relying on these solvents in manufacturing or cleaning processes should not treat the extension as a permanent reprieve.
6-Month Trend
The dominant theme over the past six months is enforcement deceleration under the current administration, with EPA repeatedly extending or postponing compliance deadlines for major Section 6 rules (TCE, perchloroethylene, carbon tetrachloride) rather than advancing new restrictions. Regulatory momentum has slowed significantly, reflecting a pattern of rollback-friendly actions and industry accommodation. At the same time, advocacy groups are mobilizing hard against any legislative weakening of TSCA, signaling a contested political environment that could shift direction if Congress or courts intervene.
Last 30 Days
The past 30 days have been highly active on the administrative side: EPA published statements of findings for new chemicals reviewed in January 2026, extended compliance deadlines for perchloroethylene and carbon tetrachloride, and floated the CDR information collection renewal. The formaldehyde composite wood products proposal is the single most operationally relevant filing for a windows/doors manufacturer and warrants immediate review. No new enforcement actions were identified in this window, consistent with the broader deceleration pattern.
Strategic Priorities
  • Review the EPA proposed formaldehyde emission standard update for composite wood products immediately — submit comments during the public comment period if any components in your product line are affected, and begin flagging suppliers who provide composite wood elements for potential re-qualification under the new QC test method.
  • Audit internal use of TCE, perchloroethylene, and carbon tetrachloride in manufacturing and maintenance operations now, while the compliance deadline extensions provide runway — document substitution options and build a phase-out timeline so you are not caught flat-footed when EPA reinstates deadlines or a new administration accelerates enforcement.
  • Track the CDR renewal process and confirm whether your manufacturing volumes for any TSCA-listed chemicals cross Section 8 reporting thresholds — the renewal notice is the early signal that the next CDR cycle is being structured, and missed filings carry significant penalty exposure.

Company Impact
Products If TCE, PCE, or CCl4 are used in fluoropolymer coating application or surface preparation processes, the extended but still-active Section 6 restrictions require immediate verification that operations either qualify under a Section 6(g) exemption or have already transitioned to compliant alternatives. Supply Chain Suppliers of cleaning solvents, degreasers, adhesives, and coating process chemicals containing TCE, PCE, or CCl4 face active TSCA Section 6 restrictions with imminent compliance deadlines, creating potential supply disruption risk for any manufacturing process inputs relying on those chemistries. Actions
  • Audit all manufacturing and coating processes for use of TCE, PCE, or CCl4; confirm whether any use falls under an active Section 6(g) exemption and document compliance with the exemption conditions before the ~May 19, 2026 TCE postponement expiration.
  • Verify CDR Section 8(a) reporting obligations for any chemical substances manufactured or imported above threshold quantities, and confirm readiness for the ICR renewal cycle ahead of the October 2026 approval expiration.
Supplier
  • Issue targeted inquiries to solvent, degreaser, and process chemical suppliers to confirm whether their TCE-, PCE-, or CCl4-containing products will remain available under TSCA Section 6 compliance timelines or require substitution, and request written confirmation of their compliance status.
  • Add TSCA Section 6 solvent restriction status to the supplier data-collection scope in the Assent platform, alongside the existing PFAS/PRISM declaration campaign, to capture supply risk from TCE/PCE/CCl4 reformulations in a single outreach cycle.
