◆ AI Intelligence Summary — PFAS
New Mexico has enacted sweeping PFAS labeling obligations effective January 1, 2027, joining Minnesota's landmark Amara's Law (PRISM registration deadline July 1, 2026) in creating state-level PFAS restrictions. The regulatory landscape is accelerating: EPA is expanding PFAS reporting requirements under TSCA, multiple state AGs (Tennessee, Michigan, New Jersey) are pursuing enforcement actions against PFAS manufacturers, and federal court challenges to EPA drinking water standards continue. Multi-state PFAS compliance is now a critical operational requirement, not a future consideration.
Company Impact

DIRECT: Your fluoropolymer coating products containing PFAS must be registered in Minnesota PRISM by July 1, 2026; New Mexico's January 1, 2027 labeling rule will require product labeling or reformulation decisions for any PFAS-containing products sold there. INDIRECT (supply chain): The July 1, 2026 Minnesota deadline creates cascading risk—if your suppliers' upstream manufacturers (domestic and foreign) fail to register PRISM, those components become illegal to distribute in Minnesota starting July 2, 2026, potentially disrupting electronics, seals, gaskets, lubricants, and adhesives sourcing. New Mexico's labeling requirement will create parallel supply chain verification demands across your component and material suppliers.

Key Risks

CRITICAL: Minnesota PRISM deadline is 18 months away; failure to register direct PFAS products or failure to secure supplier commitments to register creates operational shutdown risk in a key market. SECONDARY: New Mexico labeling rule (13 months out) applies to products sold there; absence of a clear labeling strategy now means either rapid reformulation or market exit decisions under time pressure. TERTIARY: Evolving EPA TRI reporting (2027 submission deadline) and potential future federal use restrictions could require additional disclosure and reformulation cycles. Supply chain visibility gaps—you may not know which purchased components contain PFAS upstream until suppliers respond to your education campaigns.

Next Steps

IMMEDIATE (Next 30 days): Finalize PRISM registration timeline for all direct PFAS products; confirm Assent platform has captured all fluoropolymer coating SKUs and PFAS substance declarations. Accelerate supplier outreach campaign—send second-wave targeted emails to electronics, seal/gasket, lubricant, and adhesive suppliers with explicit PRISM deadline language and request PFAS declarations by Q2 2026. WITHIN 90 DAYS: Map New Mexico product sales and initiate reformulation vs. labeling decisions for PFAS-containing products in that state; assess foreign supplier compliance risk (particularly for electronics and automation components). Establish monthly tracker of supplier PRISM registration status and escalate non-responsive suppliers to procurement leadership.
