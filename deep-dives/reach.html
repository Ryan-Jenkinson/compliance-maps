◆ AI Intelligence Summary — REACH
The EU's REACH regulatory framework is tightening restrictions on PFAS and related substances through the European Chemicals Agency (ECHA). The RAC has adopted an opinion on a proposed REACH restriction for PFAS, signaling imminent publication of formal restriction guidance. Multiple SVHC (Substances of Very High Concern) Article 33 notification deadlines are approaching for DBDPE (November 5, 2025), BPAF, and n-hexane (February 4, 2026), requiring manufacturers and importers to notify downstream users of these substances in articles.
Company Impact

If your company exports fluoropolymer coatings or PFAS-containing components to EU customers, you face direct REACH Article 33 notification obligations for any SVHC-listed PFAS in your products—failure to notify triggers supply disruption and legal liability in EU markets. Indirectly, if your suppliers (especially foreign manufacturers of electronics, seals, lubricants, and adhesives) contain DBDPE, BPAF, or n-hexane as SVHC substances, they must notify you by the November 2025 and February 2026 deadlines; non-compliance by suppliers could disqualify their products from EU sale and create cascading sourcing risk for you as a buyer of EU-destined components. The pending PFAS restriction opinion will likely accelerate toward a formal ban timeline, which could eliminate entire product categories or raw materials your company depends on.

Key Risks

The November 5, 2025 DBDPE Article 33 deadline is imminent (within months); failure to notify EU customers by this date exposes you to REACH enforcement action and loss of EU market access. The February 4, 2026 deadlines for BPAF and n-hexane create overlapping compliance burdens and increase likelihood of missed notifications if your supplier data collection is incomplete. A formal PFAS restriction published by ECHA could supersede Article 33 notification and impose outright bans on manufacturing or importing PFAS articles into the EU, eliminating revenue streams and forcing immediate reformulation decisions. Suppliers unfamiliar with REACH Article 33 obligations may not proactively notify you of SVHC content in their products, leaving you unaware of hidden compliance exposure in your supply chain.

Next Steps

Immediately audit your exported products and raw materials for DBDPE, BPAF, and n-hexane content; if present, prepare and send REACH Article 33 notifications to all EU customers and distributors before November 5, 2025 (11-month deadline is critical). Simultaneously, send targeted supplier questionnaires to all foreign manufacturers of electronics, seals, lubricants, and adhesives asking for SVHC declarations and Article 33 notification confirmation; prioritize suppliers selling into EU markets. Request advance copies of the ECHA PFAS restriction opinion (expected publication) from your trade association or legal counsel to assess whether your direct PFAS coating products will face manufacturing or import bans, and trigger reformulation timeline planning if necessary. Establish a 60-day supplier response tracking system to capture SVHC declarations ahead of February 4, 2026, and escalate any non-respondents to procurement leadership.
