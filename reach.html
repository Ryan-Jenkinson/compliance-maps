● Breaking
The February 4, 2026 simultaneous addition of the fluorinated bisphenol BPAF and n-hexane to the SVHC Candidate List is the most urgent development — REACH Article 33 supplier notification obligations for articles containing these substances above 0.1% w/w are already in effect, and EU customers have the right to request substance information within 45 days of inquiry.
6-Month Trend
ECHA has maintained steady SVHC Candidate List activity over the past six months, adding three substances across two update cycles — a pace consistent with ECHA's ongoing SVHC identification program. The dominant theme is dual-track concern: one addition targets a legacy flame retardant (DBDPE, a brominated alternative that regulators are now catching up with), while the February 2026 additions flag a fluorinated bisphenol with direct PFAS-adjacency relevance. No deceleration is visible; ECHA continues executing its roadmap under the Chemicals Strategy for Sustainability.
Last 30 Days
No new regulatory filings or enforcement actions are captured in the last 30 days beyond the February 2026 Candidate List additions already flagged as breaking news. The pipeline is quiet at the formal publication level, but the February additions are still in their immediate notification and communication obligation window — supplier outreach should be active right now. No authorization or restriction dossier decisions were recorded in this period.
Strategic Priorities
  • Immediately query all fluoropolymer and fluorinated coating suppliers via the Assent platform to confirm presence or absence of BPAF (4,4'-[2,2,2-trifluoro-1-(trifluoromethyl)ethylidene]diphenol) in their formulations — this is not a future action, Article 33 obligations are live now.
  • Issue an urgent SVHC Candidate List update notice to all EU-facing component and raw material suppliers requiring updated Declarations of Conformity covering all three February and November 2025 additions (BPAF, n-hexane, DBDPE); set a 30-day response deadline with escalation to procurement if unresponsive.
  • Cross-reference the BPAF addition with your ongoing MN PRISM PFAS registration work — if BPAF is confirmed present in any coating, it is almost certainly a PFAS reportable substance under Minnesota's definition and must be flagged to the PFAS compliance workstream immediately to avoid a gap between REACH and state-level PFAS obligations.

Company Impact
Products Bisphenol AF's addition is directly material: it is commonly used as a crosslinker in fluoroelastomers and fluoropolymer coatings — review all fluoropolymer-coated product formulations and confirm with raw material suppliers whether Bisphenol AF is present above 0.1% w/w in any article supplied into EU channels. Supply Chain Purchased electronics and automation components (DBDPE flame retardant risk), fluoroelastomer O-rings, gaskets, and seals (Bisphenol AF crosslinker risk), and industrial solvents or cleaning agents (n-hexane risk) all require updated SVHC declarations from upstream manufacturers. Actions
  • Audit fluoropolymer coating raw material supply chain immediately to determine whether Bisphenol AF is used as a crosslinker in any product formulation sold into EU markets, and update product data sheets and REACH Article 33 disclosures accordingly.
  • Update internal SVHC tracking in Assent (or equivalent platform) to include all three newly listed substances (n-hexane, Bisphenol AF, DBDPE) and flag any product lines or purchased components with confirmed or probable presence above 0.1% w/w.
Supplier
  • Issue formal SVHC update notifications to all EU-based and EU-exporting suppliers of electronics, seals/gaskets/O-rings, solvents, and adhesives, requiring revised Declarations of Conformity confirming whether n-hexane, Bisphenol AF, or DBDPE are present above 0.1% w/w in supplied articles.
  • For any supplier that cannot confirm SVHC status within 45 days, escalate to their upstream manufacturer and flag the component for qualification of an alternative source to protect continuity of supply.
