Weekly Briefing — Monday

Week of Mar 21 – 27

March 21, 2026 – March 27, 2026

A quiet week on new developments — everything in the pipeline this week is carried over. The regulatory posture remains elevated on PFAS across both the direct and supply chain sides, with PRISM the dominant near-term pressure point.

Key Developments

  • MPCA's PRISM Supplemental Guide for complex scenarios is the most actionable carried-over item this week — if anyone on the Assent collection workstream has flagged edge cases around product category classification or intentional-use determinations, this guide should be the first stop before escalating to MPCA directly.
  • Wisconsin's unanimous Senate passage of PFAS legislation is worth tracking for supply chain implications: if signed by the Governor, Wisconsin could layer new obligations on manufacturers selling into the Midwest, potentially expanding the universe of PRISM-adjacent registration requirements your suppliers' upstream manufacturers need to navigate.
  • Bisphenol AF (CAS 1478-61-1) on the SVHC Candidate List is a direct flag for the fluoropolymer coatings side — this substance appears in fluoropolymer production intermediates, which means Article 33 obligations and SVHC disclosure requirements may attach to products or components in the supply chain that weren't previously tracked under REACH.
  • New Mexico's proposed PFAS Protection Act rulemaking adds another state to the disclosure/restriction map — not an immediate action item, but worth flagging to procurement as another jurisdiction your suppliers' manufacturers may soon need to register or report in, compounding the PRISM education message.
  • The Safer States multi-state 2026 roundup confirms the patchwork is accelerating: the supplier education campaign messaging should explicitly note that PRISM is one of several concurrent state obligations landing on manufacturers in 2026, which raises the stakes for any upstream manufacturer still treating this as a wait-and-see situation.

Looking Ahead

Watch for Wisconsin Governor action on the PFAS bills — a signature would trigger a concrete timeline review for Midwest supply chain exposure beyond Minnesota. On the direct side, monitor Assent declaration return rates this week; with 14 weeks to the July 1 PRISM deadline, any supplier non-response is now a gap, not a reminder.

Did You Know

The REACH SVHC Candidate List now contains over 240 substances — when it launched in 2008, it had exactly 15. Bisphenol AF, added in February, is part of a fluorinated compound cohort that ECHA has been systematically working through since 2022.
