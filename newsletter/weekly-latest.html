Weekly Briefing — Monday

Week of Mar 21 – 27

March 21, 2026 – March 27, 2026

A quiet week on the new-development front — no breaking regulatory actions since Monday. The posture remains execution-focused: PRISM deadline pressure is building, the Wisconsin PFAS bills are moving toward the governor, and the state-level patchwork continues tightening faster than federal action can consolidate it.

Key Developments

  • Wisconsin's PFAS bills cleared the Senate unanimously and are advancing — watch for governor action, which would add another neighboring-state framework to track alongside Minnesota. No immediate operational trigger, but if bills include reporting or product restrictions, distributor and supplier obligations could mirror MN dynamics within 12–18 months.
  • New Mexico's PFAS Protection Act fact sheet was revised in February, signaling the Environment Department is actively refining implementation guidance ahead of enforcement — if any product lines ship into NM, the applicable category prohibitions need a second look now rather than at effective date.
  • ECHA's February 4 addition of Bisphenol AF and its salts to the SVHC Candidate List is directly relevant to any fluoropolymer-adjacent chemistry in the supply chain — this is a fluorinated substance, and EU-destined components or REACH compliance representations from suppliers need to account for it in Article 0.1% threshold communications.
  • The Assent webinar resource on 2026 PFAS compliance is worth routing to any supplier contacts who are stalling on declarations — it's a credible, vendor-neutral-enough framing that can supplement the outreach campaign without the company carrying the entire education burden.
  • EPA's TRI PFAS expansion (final rule, February 27) takes effect for reporting year 2026 — if any manufacturing operations release or manage listed PFAS, the first affected TRI report will be due July 1, 2027, so the clock on internal data collection has started.

Looking Ahead

Wisconsin governor action on the PFAS bills is the next concrete milestone to watch — passage would immediately prompt a gap analysis against MN Amara's Law preparedness to see what transfers. On the PRISM side, the 100-day mark before the July 1 deadline hits in early April; that's the practical last call for supplier escalation before registration timelines become unworkable.

Did You Know

The TRI program has been tracking toxic chemical releases since 1987 — it took nearly 40 years and an explicit statutory mandate from the 2020 NDAA before PFAS were formally added to the list, despite being among the most widely tracked environmental contaminants of the past two decades.
