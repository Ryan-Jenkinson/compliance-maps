Weekly Briefing — Monday

Week of Mar 21 – 27

March 21, 2026 – March 27, 2026

The week of March 21 is light on breaking developments — one new item of note in New Mexico — but the PRISM clock is ticking loudly in the background with the supplemental guide now published and Q2 in sight.

Key Developments

  • New Mexico finalized PFAS labeling requirements for consumer products on March 23, joining the growing roster of states building product-level disclosure frameworks. Direct side: assess whether any finished goods sold into NM trigger labeling obligations. Indirect side: flag to the supplier campaign team — distributors selling into NM face new disclosure pressure, which may accelerate upstream manufacturer transparency generally.
  • MPCA's PRISM supplemental guide (carried over but still the most operationally urgent item this week) is worth a close read against your edge-case product lines — the scenario-based format is designed exactly for the ambiguous registration situations you're likely still resolving. Share relevant scenarios with the Assent team to ensure data collection requests are scoped correctly.
  • The dual EPA TRI PFAS moves — final rule adding PFAS to TRI for RY2026 while simultaneously proposing burden reduction — create a short-term reporting planning problem: facilities need to prepare for full TRI obligations now while the relief proposal is still pending, with no guarantee the changes land before the July reporting cycle.
  • Bisphenol AF's addition to the SVHC Candidate List (carried over) warrants a specific supply chain check this week: it's used in fluoropolymer and specialty polymer production, meaning it could appear in the same upstream supply chains already under PFAS scrutiny — your Assent declarations should be flagging this substance explicitly alongside PFAS inquiries.

Looking Ahead

Watch for Wisconsin's PFAS bills to move to the House and potentially reach the governor — unanimous Senate passage suggests momentum, and any enacted provisions will extend the multi-state compliance perimeter further. On the PRISM front, the end of March marks roughly 90 days to the July 1 deadline; expect MPCA to increase communications in April, which is a natural hook for your next supplier campaign push.

Did You Know

New Mexico's new labeling rule means that as of March 2026, more than 15 states have enacted some form of product-level PFAS disclosure or restriction — compared to zero state-level product PFAS laws before 2021. Five years, zero to fifteen.
