Weekly Briefing — Tuesday

Week of Mar 21 – 27

March 21, 2026 – March 27, 2026

All items this week are carried over — no new developments broke today. The posture is steady-state monitoring: PRISM is live and accepting submissions, the ECHA PFAS restriction opinion is imminent, and the supplier education campaign remains the most time-sensitive operational lever heading into Q2.

Key Developments

  • PRISM is operational and MPCA's supplemental guide is published — the "waiting for the portal" excuse is gone. Direct-side: any remaining product scope decisions and Assent declaration pulls need to accelerate now, not in May. Indirect-side: this is the concrete proof point to drop into supplier outreach — manufacturers have no procedural barrier to registering today.
  • ECHA's RAC opinion on the universal PFAS restriction is adopted and publication is imminent — once public, this will firm up the restriction timeline and scope, directly affecting fluoropolymer-coated component exports and supply chain sourcing strategy for any EU-destined product lines.
  • Bisphenol AF (a fluorinated PFAS-adjacent substance) and n-hexane are now on the SVHC Candidate List as of February 4 — run both through Assent against current component declarations; Bisphenol AF in particular warrants a targeted pull given its fluorinated structure and overlap with PFAS-adjacent chemistries.
  • New Mexico finalized its PFAS labeling rule and is now actively questioning its fluoropolymer exemption via legislative memorial — this is the clearest signal yet that fluoropolymer carve-outs at the state level are under pressure; worth flagging to leadership as a reformulation business case input beyond MN alone.
  • The EPA's proposed TSCA 8(a)(7) narrowing — adding de minimis and imported articles exemptions — remains proposed, not final; do not adjust any reporting assumptions yet, but monitor for finalization as it would materially reduce the indirect supply chain reporting burden on imported PFAS-containing components.

Looking Ahead

Watch for ECHA's formal publication of the RAC PFAS restriction opinion — that document will contain the scope definitions and derogation criteria that determine whether your fluoropolymer coatings qualify for any use-specific exemption. On the domestic side, Wisconsin's PFAS legislation moving unanimously through the Senate bears watching for any product-sales provisions that could expand the Minnesota-style compliance map into a neighboring state.

Did You Know

Fun fact: New Mexico's PFAS Protection Act is one of the few state PFAS statutes that explicitly built a fluoropolymer exemption into the original text — and the legislature is now questioning it before the ink is fully dry. Regulatory carve-outs have a shorter half-life than the substances they were meant to protect.
