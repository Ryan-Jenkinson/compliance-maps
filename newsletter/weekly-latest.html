Weekly Briefing — Sunday

Week of Mar 21 – 27

March 21, 2026 – March 27, 2026

A quiet week for truly new developments — everything in the feed is carried over from earlier in the week, with no breaking items landing before Sunday. The PRISM supplemental guide from MPCA remains the most operationally significant piece in the queue.

Key Developments

  • MPCA released a supplemental PRISM reporting guide addressing specific edge-case scenarios manufacturers and sellers encounter during registration — pull this now and cross-reference against your current product list and any supplier declarations coming in through Assent; ambiguous reporting situations (e.g., component-level PFAS vs. intentional addition) are likely addressed.
  • ECHA's February 4 addition of n-hexane and BPAF to the SVHC Candidate List remains live and actionable — Article 33 obligations are triggered for articles above 0.1% w/w, and BPAF specifically is used as a monomer in fluoropolymer and high-performance polymer systems, meaning both your direct product side and inbound components from suppliers could be implicated.
  • The Assent PFAS webinar on 2026 compliance rules, including PRISM, is still on the table — worth confirming whether the compliance team or supplier-facing contacts are registered, particularly given the supplier education campaign already in motion.
  • The wave of 2026 state PFAS restrictions (Safer States coverage) and New Mexico's proposed rules collectively signal that the supplier education campaign scope may need to extend beyond MN-specific PRISM messaging — distributors selling into multiple states face a patchwork, and framing your outreach around the broader state trend strengthens urgency.
  • Wisconsin's unanimous Senate passage of PFAS bills is worth a low-level watch: unanimous votes tend to move through remaining chambers quickly, and Wisconsin suppliers in your base may soon face disclosure or restriction obligations that affect what they can sell you.

Looking Ahead

Watch for any MPCA clarification or FAQ follow-up to the supplemental PRISM guide as manufacturers begin testing the portal against real product scenarios — edge cases submitted by industry often prompt additional agency guidance in the weeks following. On REACH, confirm whether any articles containing BPAF are in your inbound supply chain before the Article 33 communication window becomes a sourcing conversation.

Did You Know

BPAF (Bisphenol AF) was first synthesized in the 1960s as a monomer for heat-resistant polymers — it took roughly 60 years and a formal SVHC listing for regulators to formally require anyone to tell their customers it was in their product.
