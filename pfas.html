EPA announced on March 25 that it is expanding PFAS toxic chemical reporting under TRI while simultaneously proposing changes to make reporting 'more practical and implementable' — review the proposed changes immediately to assess whether any threshold or de minimis adjustments affect your fluoropolymer coating operations' RY2026 filing obligations.
6-Month Trend
PFAS regulatory activity is accelerating on multiple fronts simultaneously — federal TRI reporting expansion, state-level use prohibitions, and the Minnesota PRISM registration deadline are all converging in the first half of 2026. The dominant theme is disclosure and accountability: regulators are forcing manufacturers, distributors, and importers to surface PFAS use in products and releases before enforcement escalates. The Trump EPA is threading a needle — expanding TRI transparency while simultaneously proposing burden-reduction tweaks — signaling that PFAS regulation is politically durable regardless of administration.
Last 30 Days
The last 30 days have been dominated by EPA's dual announcements on March 25: TRI expansion for PFAS goes into effect for RY2026, and EPA is simultaneously proposing modifications to reduce reporting burden — watch for the proposed rule comment period opening, which is an actionable engagement opportunity. Minnesota MPCA published updated PRISM guidance, prohibition lists, and a communications toolkit in the same window, indicating the agency is in final-push mode ahead of July 1. No enforcement actions or deadline extensions have been signaled.
Strategic Priorities
  • Immediately audit your fluoropolymer coating process volumes against the newly finalized TRI PFAS reporting thresholds for RY2026 — determine whether a Form R filing obligation exists and assign an owner; this is separate from PRISM and has its own EPA reporting calendar.
  • Accelerate the supplier PRISM registration campaign with a hard 60-day response deadline (end of May): segment your supply base by risk tier (foreign manufacturers of seals/gaskets/electronics at top), escalate non-responders to procurement holds, and document outreach for defensible due diligence if a supplier fails to register and you face a procurement disruption.
  • Pull the newly published MN MPCA PRISM supplemental guide and 2025 prohibition schedule immediately — cross-reference your current PRISM registration list against the prohibition list to confirm no products you plan to continue using are subject to a use ban that predates or coincides with the July 1 registration deadline.

Company Impact
Products The July 1, 2026 PRISM deadline is now approximately 100 days away — fluoropolymer-coated components must be registered in PRISM immediately, with the finalized MN fee rule and updated PRISM system both active; simultaneously, the New Mexico fluoropolymer exemption review signals that exemptions the company relies on in other states may erode. Supply Chain Electronics, O-rings, gaskets, seals, lubricants, adhesives, and PPE purchased from distributors are all at risk of becoming unsaleable in Minnesota after July 1, 2026 if their upstream manufacturers — including foreign manufacturers — have not registered in PRISM. Actions
  • Confirm all fluoropolymer-coated product SKUs are actively registered or queued in PRISM by April 30, 2026 — use the MPCA PRISM supplemental guide to resolve any CUU classification ambiguities and finalize Assent-collected declarations to support submissions.
  • Assess the New Mexico fluoropolymer exemption risk now: identify which product lines currently rely on that exemption and begin contingency planning for disclosure or reformulation in the event NMED removes it following their legislative review.
Supplier
  • Immediately escalate supplier campaign from awareness emails to required response: send a formal written request to all distributors of PFAS-containing components demanding written confirmation that their manufacturers have initiated or completed PRISM registration, with a response deadline of May 15, 2026.
  • Build a PRISM registration status tracker for all critical single-source components (especially O-rings, seals, and specialty electronics) and identify alternative qualified sources now for any component whose manufacturer cannot confirm PRISM registration — do not wait until July 1.
